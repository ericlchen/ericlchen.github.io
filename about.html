Resume

I should have went to design school.

I’m a fourth year student from the Smith School of Business at Queen’s University. After my first year of business school, I quickly realized that none of the traditional career paths fit me. I began to combine my skills in web development and business strategy to do work for startups and other clients, which sparked my interest in product design. During my internship at TribalScale, my passion for design expanded across disciplines as I gained appreciation for artistic expression. Working with senior designers inspired me to gain a more holistic view of design so I could draw inspiration from every creative field.

This is when I realized that I should have went to design school. However, I stayed in business school so I could develop strong soft skills and incorporate strategic thinking frameworks in my design process. I took advantage of my school’s exchange program to live in France, travel across Europe, and become inspired by the art surrounding me.

This year, I started OLIGARCHY — a collaborative art project that raises awareness about the harmful social environment of business school through film, publishing, and art exhibitions. My goal is to create an open dialogue with the student body to talk about school and career-related issues on a public forum. I utilized my technical abilities in graphic design and my personal experiences in school to craft a self-expressive body of work I’m proud to call my visual thesis.

With my background in digital marketing and passion for design, I aim to create strategic digital experiences and visual identities. In my time at school I have designed user experiences for startups and other larger clients such as CIBC, Guestlogix, and Guardian Analytics. I also design visual identities with local artists and student businesses such as Francisco Corbett, Losers Weepers Vintage, and MUSE Magazine.

I’m currently looking to gain experience and knowledge through internship roles in fashion, music, publishing, or e-commerce.
